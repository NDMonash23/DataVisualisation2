International Travel in Australia

It's no secret that Australians love to travel. In fact, Australia ranks in the top ten nations in the world when it comes to average annual overseas trips per person, with up to 40% of Australians travelling overseas each year.

So what does international air travel in Australia look like? In this visualisation, we take a closer look at where Australians travel from, where they travel to, and how they travel.

Where do Australians travel from?

Busiest departure cities

So which cities in Australia do people travel overseas from? We would expect the major cities to have higher international travel traffic compared to smaller cities. Major cities also often have larger and more advanced airports, which makes it attractive for airlines to offer more flights out of these airports.

The bar chart on the right shows the total number of international flights at cities in Australia. As expected, we can see that the major cities host far more international flights compared to the smaller cities, making them the most popular departure cities for Australians travelling overseas.



Where do Australians travel to?



International city destinations

So which international cities do Australians visit?

The proportional symbol map on the left visualises the number of passengers that flew from Australia to different cities around the world. Use the map centre drop down menu to focus on a particular continent, and use the zoom slider to zoom in on the map.

We can see that tourist destinations such as Los Angeles, London, and Bangkok are among the most popular destinations. We can also see that cities such as Singapore, Dubai, and Doha are also visited by many Australians. However, these cities are known to be hub cities, or cities in which passengers stopover in transit to their final destination. So we can expect that for many of the passengers who flew to these hub cities, the hub city was not their final destination.

Global share of Australian flights

The treemap below visualises the global share of all international flights to and from Australia. We see that the majority of flights were between Australia and Asia, with Oceania being the next most popular area of the world. The countries with the most flights to and from Australia include Singapore, New Zealand, the United States of America, and the United Arab Emirates.

Most popular routes

The sankey diagram below shows the 20 most popular international routes from Australia. As expected, the most popular routes depart from major cities such as Sydney and Melbourne and arrive at hub cities such as Singapore and Dubai.



How do Australians travel?

Most popular airlines

So which airlines are Australians choosing for their international travel? The scatterplot below shows the number of flights departing Australia and the number of passengers carried for all airlines servicing Australia. Select a continent using the legend to filter the plot to show only airlines that have their origin at that continent. From the plot, we can see that Asian airlines are the most popular choice for flying out of Australia. Additionally, many of the popular airlines have their origin at hub cities in Asia.


Popular aircraft types

Most airlines have a variety of aircraft types which they can deploy for flights to and from Australia. If a route is popular, a larger aircraft with high capacity such as the Airbus A380 or Boeing 777 might be preferred to smaller aircrafts. If a route covers a longer distance, a modern aircraft with higher fuel efficiency such as the Airbus A350 or Boeing 787 may be chosen. The heatmap below shows the number of flights for each aircraft type at cities in Australia. We can see that both large and modern aircrafts are often deployed at the busy major cities in Australia, while smaller aircrafts fly at the smaller cities.
